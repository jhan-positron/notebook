## Gotchas & decisions
- **CLOS state resets at every boot** (BIOS reprograms the partition) — re-run `flat_freq_apply` after ANY reboot before benchmarking.
- **`turbo-freq enable` does not restore the clipped baseline** once assocs are fixed — to A/B against boot behavior you must re-associate the boot map (harness revert does this) or reboot.
- `core-power disable` is a no-op here (enable bit already reads "disabled" at boot; pcode enforces caps regardless). Per the concurrent chat's finding (in memory): `core-power enable` *wipes* the CLOS3 clamp config — don't use it casually.
- `intel-speed-select` reports success **per entity**; substring checks pass on partial success. Utils now require exact success counts (288 for the big assoc). isst also writes to stderr (see `delphi-access-notes.md`).
- TRL buckets (per 24-core domain, non-AVX): ≤3→4400, ≤20→4100, 24→3900. The 80-CPU CI load keeps 20/24 busy → 4100 is the realistic flat ceiling; 3900 is the all-core ceiling.
